<p><p>Methyl Methacrylate (MMA) is produced through various methods including the Acetone Cyanohydrin (ACH) Method, Isobutylene Method, and Ethylene Method. The ACH Method involves the reaction of acetone cyanohydrin with sulfuric acid, while the Isobutylene Method involves the oxidation of isobutylene. The Ethylene Method utilizes the oxidation of ethylene with air and acetic acid. Each method has its own advantages and disadvantages in terms of cost, efficiency, and environmental impact, leading to different market trends and demands.</p></p>
