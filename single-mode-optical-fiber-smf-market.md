<p><p>Single-Mode Optical Fiber (SMF) is a type of optical fiber with a small core size that allows only one mode of light to propagate. There are three main types of SMF market standards: ITU-T.G.652, ITU-T.G.653, and ITU-T.G.655. ITU-T.G.652 is the most common type, known for its low water peak and optimized for long-distance transmission. ITU-T.G.653 is designed for minimal dispersion, while ITU-T.G.655 is optimized for increased bandwidth over long distances. These SMF market types cater to different needs in the telecommunications industry.</p></p>
