<p><p>Preclinical X-ray Imaging can be categorized into two main types: Structural Imaging and Functional Imaging. On the other hand, Functional Imaging measures biological processes and functions within the body, enabling researchers to understand the dynamics and interactions of various systems. Both types of imaging play a crucial role in preclinical research, offering valuable information for drug development and disease modeling.</p></p>
