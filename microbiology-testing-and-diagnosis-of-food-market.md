<p><p>Microbiology testing in the food market involves the analysis of indicator microorganisms, pathogens, and toxins to ensure food safety. Indicator microorganisms such as E. coli and Salmonella are used to assess the overall cleanliness and potential contamination levels of food products. Pathogens testing identifies harmful bacteria like Listeria and Campylobacter that can cause foodborne illnesses. Toxins testing focuses on detecting harmful substances produced by bacteria, such as botulinum toxin in canned foods. These tests are crucial for ensuring the quality and safety of food products.</p></p>
