<p><p>Transfection reagents are used to introduce nucleic acids into cells for research and therapeutic purposes. Biochemical transfection involves the use of lipid-based or polymer-based carriers to deliver nucleic acids into cells. Physical transfection, on the other hand, utilizes physical methods such as electroporation or microinjection to achieve the same goal. Both types of transfection have their advantages and disadvantages, and researchers may choose a method based on the specific requirements of their experiment.</p></p>
