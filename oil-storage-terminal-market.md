<p><p>Oil storage terminals can be categorized into two main types: strategic reserve and commercial reserve markets. Strategic reserve terminals are owned and operated by governments to store oil in case of emergencies or disruptions in supply. These terminals are crucial for national security and energy planning. On the other hand, commercial reserve terminals are privately owned facilities used by oil companies and traders to store oil for trading purposes. These terminals play a key role in ensuring a stable supply of oil in the market and managing price fluctuations.</p></p>
